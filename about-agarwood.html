침향 (沈香) · Aquilaria · Oud

What is Agarwood?

The dark, resinous heartwood formed inside Aquilaria trees — considered more precious than gold and revered across cultures for over 3,000 years.

The Essence

A Wound That Creates Wonder

Agarwood — known as 침향 (沈香) in Korean and Chinese medicine — forms when specific Aquilaria trees are wounded by fungal infection or physical damage. In response, the tree saturates its injured heartwood with a dark, aromatic resin. Over decades, this resin hardens into one of the rarest, most valuable natural substances on Earth.

The result is a wood so dense it sinks in water — its name literally meaning "sinking wood" (沈香). It has been traded along the Silk Road, burned in royal palaces, and documented in the world's oldest medical texts. Today, the finest Grade A agarwood commands over $100,000 per kilogram.

Formation Process

How Agarwood is Born

Four natural stages transform a living tree into the world's rarest aromatic material.

01
Wound
Natural or artificial damage to the Aquilaria tree — from insect attack, fungal penetration, or deliberate inoculation — triggers the tree's defensive response.
02
Infection
Phialophora parasitica and other fungi colonise the wound site. The deeper the microbial penetration, the richer and more complex the resulting resin.
03
Resin Secretion
The tree saturates injured wood with dark oleoresin — a chemical defence that also gives agarwood its legendary fragrance, density, and medicinal properties.
04
Maturation
Over 10 to 40+ years the resin-saturated wood hardens completely. The longer the maturation, the higher the grade — and the greater its value.

Unique Characteristics

Four Properties That Make It Irreplaceable

No synthetic compound has ever replicated the full sensory and therapeutic complexity of natural agarwood.

🌿
Unique Fragrance
Deep, complex, and impossible to replicate synthetically. Agarwood's scent profile — woody, balsamic, sweet, and animalic — has been prized in perfumery for millennia. It promotes mental and physical wellness through aromatherapy.
💎
Extreme Rarity
Only 2% of wild Aquilaria trees naturally produce agarwood resin. The decades-long formation process, combined with historic overharvesting, makes high-grade specimens extraordinarily scarce and valuable.
⚖️
Worth More Than Gold
Grade A wild agarwood commands $50,000–$100,000+ per kilogram — far exceeding the price of gold. It is considered the world's most expensive natural raw material among aromatic substances.
📜
3,000 Years of Tradition
Documented in Li Shizhen's Bencaogangmu, Heo Jun's Donguibogam, ancient Egyptian texts, and the Goryeo Dynasty's Maehyangbi records. Used across East Asia, the Middle East, and India for medicine, ritual, and royalty.

Historical Journey

3,000 Years of Sacred Use

From ancient Egypt to the courts of Joseon Korea, agarwood has been central to medicine, spirituality, and luxury culture across every major civilisation.

Ancient Egypt · 1,000 BCE
Royal Embalming & Ritual
Ancient Egyptians imported oud resin along early trade routes for use in royal embalming and sacred ritual burning in temple ceremonies.
Han Dynasty · 200 BCE – 200 CE
Silk Road Trade
Agarwood became one of the most valuable commodities on the Silk Road, traded from Southeast Asian forests to Chinese imperial courts and Arab markets.
Goryeo Dynasty · 918–1392
Korean Maehyangbi Culture
Agarwood played a central role in Buddhist Korea — documented in the Maehyangbi burial records as a spiritual offering and healing medium.
Joseon Dynasty · 1392–1897
Royal Medical Records
Consumption of agarwood by Joseon royalty is documented in official Sillok annals. Heo Jun's Donguibogam (UNESCO heritage) details its medicinal applications.
Ming Dynasty · 1593
Bencaogangmu Classification
After 27 years of research, Li Shizhen classified agarwood's medicinal properties in the world's most comprehensive pre-modern pharmacopoeia.
Today · Global
Modern Science Confirms Ancient Wisdom
Contemporary research validates agarwood's anti-inflammatory, anxiolytic, and neuroactive properties, bridging millennia of traditional knowledge with modern medicine.

Grading System

Understanding Agarwood Grades

Grade Name Characteristics Typical Use
S+ Kynam / 기남 Densest resin, >90% saturation, sinks instantly. Rarest on Earth. Collector, royal gift, investment
A Super Grade 70–90% resin, rich dark colour, complex fragrance, sinks in water. Premium oud oil, high-end incense
B Grade 1 50–70% resin. Strong fragrance, partially sinkers. Vietnam/Cambodia origin. Bakhoor, medicinal extract, capsules
C Grade 2 30–50% resin. Good fragrance profile. Widely used in incense blends. Incense sticks, aromatherapy, tea
D Grade 3 10–30% resin. Light colour, mild scent. Cultivated plantation wood. Fragrance blending, accessories
