What is Agarwood?

A Tree's Wound Becomes the World's Rarest Gift

Agarwood forms when an Aquilaria tree secretes resin to protect itself from injury. Over decades, this resin hardens into dark, fragrant heartwood — more precious than gold. Used in traditional medicine for over 3,000 years, it remains one of the most sought-after natural substances on Earth.

01
Wound
Natural or artificial damage to the Aquilaria tree triggers a protective response.
02
Infection
Fungal and microbial penetration at the wound site intensifies resin production.
03
Resin Secretion
The tree saturates injured wood with dark, aromatic oleoresin as a defence.
04
Maturation
Over 10–40 years, resin hardens into prized agarwood — fragrant, dark, and dense.
